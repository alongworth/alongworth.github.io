Home care packages

If you need help to manage your day to day life, a Home Care Package might be right for you. A Home Care Package is a package of services for older people and people with a disability that can give you the help you need to keep on living independently in your home.

What is on offer:

  • Help around the house
  • Personal care
  • Assistance with transport
  • Understand how your HCP is funded
  • Meal services

The best thing about the packages is that they are tailored to suit your own needs. They asses your needs and take your cultural background and unique circumstances into consideration.

How do I qualify?

For people over 65, you will need to be assessed first byÂ an Aged Care Assessment Services (ACAS).

You can be referred to Aged Care Assessment Services for an assessment by your:

  • Doctor
  • Community care service provider
  • Family members
  • Health professionals; or
  • You can ask for a referral yourself.

There is no minimum age for accessing Home Care Packages if you have a disability.

What is the process?

  1. The ACAS team will look at how you are currently managing at home.
  2. You will receive a letter about which level of home care has been approved for you:
    • Level 1 supports people with basic care needs
    • Level 2 supports people with low-level care needs
    • Level 3 supports people with intermediate care needs
    • Level 4 supports people with high-level care needs
  3. Once you choose AMCS as your provider, you can decide on the specifics for your individual services package.
